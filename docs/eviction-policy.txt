  For this part of the extra credit, we decided to implement RRIP in both the TLB and the L2 cache. We knew that implementing RRIP in the TLB was kind of dumb
  because we didn't have to do a page table walk, but we decided to do it anyways to make sure we understood how RRIP worked. As for the L2, by looking
  at the MPKI differences between the LRU and the RRIP, we noticed that RRIP was a decent amount better than LRU.
  
  RRIP is better for larger workloads with lower temporal locality, where you scan large amounts of data that you may or may not use, which these traces do as it is
  a matrix operation. RRIP excels in this regard, leading to RRIP being better than LRU regarding misses.
